Support & Help

Getting Started

Dugout Score is a game scoring app for Little League Minors baseball. Here's how to get up and running:

  • Set up your roster — Tap the menu (≡) → Roster. Enter your team name, then add each player with their name, jersey number, and age (7–12). You can change a player's age anytime by tapping the underlined Age N on their row.
  • Create a game — Tap + New Game on the home screen. Enter the date, opponent name, and whether you're home or away. Reorder the batting lineup if needed, then tap Start Game.
  • Score the game — Use the Ball, Strike, Foul, and In Play buttons to track each pitch. Or switch to Quick mode for faster scoring with Hit, Walk, Out, K, Run, and Error buttons.
  • View stats — Tap Stats in the menu for season totals, or tap any completed game on the home screen to see per-game stats, at-bat logs, and position assignments.

Rules-Aware Scoring New in 1.1

The scoring engine enforces official Little League Minor Division rules. When a rule-based end condition is met, you'll see a prompt explaining the rule (with the official citation) and two choices:

ConditionRuleYour choices
5 runs scored in a half-inningRule 5.07End Half-Inning / Keep Playing
Full roster has batted onceRule 5.07End Half-Inning / Keep Playing
Walk-off winRule 4.11(c)End Game / Keep Playing
15-run lead after 3 inningsRule 4.10(e)End Game / Keep Playing
10-run lead after 4 inningsRule 4.10(e)End Game / Keep Playing
8-run lead after 5 inningsRule 4.10(e)End Game / Keep Playing
6 innings complete (not tied)Rule 4.10(a)End Game / Keep Playing (extras)

"Keep Playing" is there for when the umpire or coaches don't call the rule. The app won't re-prompt for the same condition in the same half-inning or game. Three outs always ends the half automatically — no prompt needed.

Pitch Count Tracking

Your Team's Pitchers

When your team is fielding, tap the OUR PITCHER card to select the current pitcher. Every pitch is counted. The card shows the running count with the age-based limit:

League AgeMax Pitches / Game
7–850
9–1075
11–1285

The count turns orange at 80% of the limit and red at the limit. The Stats → Pitching tab shows required rest days and the earliest date each pitcher is available.

Opponent Pitchers New

When your team is batting, tap the OPP PITCHER card to enter the current opponent pitcher's jersey number. The app tracks their pitch count for the game. You can switch numbers at any time (e.g., when a reliever enters); each pitcher's count is tracked separately.

Mound Visits New in 1.1

The Visit button in the bottom toolbar logs a mound visit. Minor League limits (Rule 8.06):

  • Per inning: 2 visits (3rd visit = pitcher must be removed)
  • Per game: 3 visits (4th visit = pitcher must be removed)

The button turns red when you've hit either limit. The per-inning counter resets when a new inning starts.

Intentional Walk New in 1.1

The IBB button (in pitch-by-pitch mode) issues an intentional walk per Rule 6.08(a)(2). It adds the correct number of balls to the active pitcher's pitch count, walks the batter, and forces runners on filled bases.

Correcting Mistakes

Undo

Tap the ← N button to see the labeled undo history (up to 40 actions deep). Each entry shows what happened (e.g., "Ball 3", "Single", "Stolen Base"). Tap "Undo this" to roll back the most recent action. Undo restores the full game state.

Edit Scoreboard

Tap any inning score cell on the live scoreboard to adjust runs with +/− buttons. Each adjustment is undoable.

Change Batter

Tap "Change" on the At Bat card to skip to a different player in the lineup.

Reopen a Finished Game New

If a game was ended by mistake or you need to make corrections:

  • Immediately after ending: Tap ← Undo End Game on the FINAL screen.
  • Later: Open the game from Home → Game Stats → Reopen Game.

All scoring state is preserved. Only one game can be live at a time.

Baserunner Management

  • Steal buttons (→2nd, →3rd, →Home) appear when a destination base is open.
  • CS/PO button on your runners records Caught Stealing or Picked Off.
  • Opponent runners are tracked as "OPP" on the diamond. Tap "Out" to remove them.
  • Bases clear automatically when the half-inning changes.

Defensive Positions & Mandatory Play

Tap Pos during a live game to assign positions (P, C, 1B, 2B, 3B, SS, LF, CF, RF, EH) per player per half-inning. The Game Stats → Pos tab shows the full position grid and includes a mandatory play indicator that flags any player with fewer than 2 half-innings on defense. New in 1.1

Built-In Rules Reference New in 1.1

Tap menu → Rules, or the book icon during a live game, to open a searchable reference of every official Little League Minor Division rule that governs the app. Six chapters cover Game Length, Half-Inning End, Batting, Baserunning, Pitching, and Mandatory Play. Each rule includes the official citation and an explanation of how the app implements it.

Game Timer

The timer auto-starts when a game is created. Tap ▸/⏸ to pause/resume. It turns orange at 90 minutes and red at 105 minutes (the local-option time limit per Rule 4.10(c)(2)). The timer persists across app restarts.

Stats & Trends

Per-game stats are available in four tabs: Hitting (AB, H, 1B–HR, RBI, BB, K, SB, AVG), Fielding (PO, A, E, FP%), Log (at-bat play-by-play), and Pos (positions + mandatory play). Season aggregates include OBP, SLG, pitching availability, and W-L-T record.

The Trends view shows game-over-game line charts for team runs and opponent runs. Select any player to see their cumulative batting average trend.

PDF Reports

  • Per-game: Tap PDF in Game Stats. Includes line score, hitting, fielding, pitch counts, at-bat log, and position assignments.
  • Season: Tap Season PDF in Stats. Includes W-L-T record, game log, cumulative hitting and fielding.

Both generate a print-ready page. Use Share → Print → Save as PDF on iPhone.

Data Management

  • Export: Home screen → Export. Saves all data (roster, games, stats) as a JSON file.
  • Import: Home screen → Import. Supports both the current format and legacy backups from the original HTML version of the app.

Tip: Always export a backup before updating the app.

Dark & Light Mode

Tap the menu and select Light Mode / Dark Mode to toggle. Dark mode uses a deep green theme (great for evening games); light mode has a high-contrast background for direct sunlight.

Requirements

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I close the app during a game?

The full game state is saved after every action. When you reopen the app, the live game card appears on the Home screen — tap it to resume exactly where you left off.

The umpire didn't call the mercy rule / run cap. What do I do?

Tap "Keep Playing" when the prompt appears. The app will continue scoring without re-prompting for the same condition.

Can I have two games going at once?

No. Only one game can be live at a time. Finish or end the current game before starting a new one.

How accurate are pitch counts in Quick mode?

Each Quick-mode action counts as 1 pitch. In reality, a walk is 4+ pitches and a strikeout is 3+. For accurate pitch counts, use Pitch mode.

Can I track stats for the opposing team's players?

The app tracks team-level runs, hits, and errors for the opponent, plus individual pitch counts by opponent pitcher number. Individual opponent batting stats are not tracked.

How do I transfer data to a new phone?

Export a JSON backup from the old phone (Home → Export). Transfer the file to the new phone (AirDrop, email, iCloud Drive, etc.). Open the app on the new phone and import the backup.

Does the app work offline?

Yes. No internet connection is needed. All data is stored locally on your device.

Privacy Policy

Dugout Score does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. All data is stored locally on your device and never leaves it unless you explicitly export a backup file. The app contains no analytics, no advertising, and no network requests.
